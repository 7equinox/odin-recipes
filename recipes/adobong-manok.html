Adobong Manok

SOURCE: Filipino Chicken Adobo Recipe

Description

Chicken adobo is chicken cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and whole peppercorns. The chicken becomes tender, and the sauce turns salty, sour, and garlicky. That is really the beauty of it. You do not need many ingredients to make something that feels complete.

Ingredients

  • Chicken – Bone in, skin on thighs and drumsticks.
  • Soy sauce – Silver Swan or Datu Puti.
  • White vinegar – Cane vinegar or any white vinegar.
  • Garlic – A lot. A whole head is normal.
  • Dried bay leaves – Two or three pieces.
  • Whole black peppercorns – About a teaspoon.
  • Sugar – Just a teaspoon.
  • Water – Enough to braise the chicken.
  • Cooking Oil – Any neutral oil for pan frying.

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken
    1. Crush the garlic using a mortar and pestle.
    2. Combine the chicken, soy sauce, and crushed garlic in a large bowl. Mix well.
    3. Marinate for at least 1 hour. The longer the chicken marinates, the better the flavor.
    4. Separate the chicken from the garlic and marinade. Set the garlic and marinade aside.
  2. Pan Fry the Chicken
    1. Heat a cooking pot and pour in 3 tablespoons of cooking oil.
    2. Once the oil gets hot, pan fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.
  3. Simmer
    1. Pour the remaining cooking oil into the same pot.. Add the garlic from the marinade. Sauté until it starts to brown.
    2. Put the chicken back into the pot. Add the remaining marinade, whole peppercorns, and dried bay leaves.
    3. Pour in the water and let it boil.
    4. Cover the pot and simmer for 30 minutes, or until the chicken becomes tender.
  4. Add Vinegar and Finish
    1. Pour in the vinegar. Let it boil before stirring. Stir and continue simmering for 15 minutes.
    2. Add the sugar and salt as needed. Stir, then turn the heat off.Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot with rice.
