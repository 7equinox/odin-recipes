Adobong Manok Recipe

Description

Some historians of Asian cuisine say that Filipinos were already cooking adobo before the Spanish arrived. Using vinegar to preserve meat is one of the oldest ways of preserving food. Pork adobo is another popular variation of this dish, and likewise. Over the years there have been many different treatments of adobo, but chicken adobo is one of the most popular for good reason!

Chicken adobo is a comforting dish loved by kids and adults alike, wherein the juicy chicken marinated in a flavorful umami-rich adobo sauce. Everyone — from kid to grown-up — is crazy for this tasty meal!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 6 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn

Steps

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. Note: the longer the time, the better
  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil.
  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.
  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender
  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
